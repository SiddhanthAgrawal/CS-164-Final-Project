Backstory

Some years ago, I was at a park where a tank was being shown. A green car was placed in the path of the tank to show the tank's abilities. The tank successfully destroyed the car, and we all clapped happily. However, after some time, I saw Mr. Bean there. He seemed quite sad. I approached him and asked what happened, and it turned out that the car was his car, and he parked it there by mistake. I consolidated him, and after some time, he was happy again. We became good friends and went on many trips. He is more of a friend to me than a pet. He is mostly happy and makes me happy too.

Explanation of Reasons behind State Transitions

Mr. Bean is a very easy going pet and friend. The only thing he wants from you is food and attention. He mostly remains happy and loves to dance. He can dance in almost any mood. If he's angry and you start dancing, he'll start dancing too. But he is a hungry pet. He needs to be fed, or he starts getting mad. Besides you, he also loves his teddy to his death. He can't leave it. He needs it when he bathes; he needs it when he is out driving. If you take his teddy, he gets sad, and if you don't return it for a longer period, he starts getting annoyed and gets angry. But he forgets things quite fast too. If he's sad and you leave him alone for some time, he'll get normal. He gets sleepy quite often too. If you feed him too much, he'll get sleepy. If he keeps dancing too much, he gets sleepy. He also is quite flirty, but he likes to remain single for now. Overall he is a great pet.

Information for Programmers

Each state has a dedicated function which is called when a state transition is take placing on pressing a button. Most of the functions call another function after some time if no button has been pressed. Each state is also given an integer value (stored in a variable named state) from 1 (Relaxed) to 10 (Not serious). These integer values are used for conditions in the functions for respnse when button is pressed. A rand() function is also defined which calls either the state function relaxed() or happy() depending on whether the random number chosen by the function call random(2) is 0 or 1.
